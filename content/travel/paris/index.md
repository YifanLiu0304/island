## Quick Guide

**Best Time to Visit**: April-June, September-October  
**Local Transport**: Metro, RER, Walking  
**Must Experience**: Louvre, Station F, Le Marais  
**Hidden Gem**: Musée Carnavalet  

## Innovation & Technology

### Station F
- **World's Largest Startup Campus**
- **International Accelerators**
- **Event Spaces & Conferences**
- _Pro tip: Public restaurant "La Felicità" inside_

### Tech Districts
- **Paris-Saclay**: Research cluster
- **Sentier**: Digital district
- **La Défense**: Business hub
- _Networking: Many tech meetups weekly_

## Cultural Heritage

### Museum Circuit
1. **Louvre**: World's largest art museum
2. **Musée d'Orsay**: Impressionist masterpieces
3. **Centre Pompidou**: Modern art
4. _Museum Pass recommended_

### Historic Districts
- **Le Marais**: Medieval architecture
- **Latin Quarter**: Academic heart
- **Montmartre**: Artists' hill
- _Best explored: Early morning walks_

## Local Experiences

### Culinary Scene
- **Rue Cler**: Market street
- **Rue Montorgueil**: Food shops
- **Marché des Enfants Rouges**: Oldest market
- _Must try: Morning croissants_

### Café Culture
- **Café de Flore**: Historic spot
- **Boot Café**: Modern coffee
- **Fragments**: Third wave coffee
- _Coffee tip: "Un café" means espresso_

## Innovation Quarters

### Startup Scene

### Research & Development
- **Paris-Saclay**: Science cluster

- _Many spaces open to public_

## Practical Tips

### Getting Around
- Metro/RER: Get Navigo pass
- Vélib': Bike sharing system
- Walking: Most central areas
- _Download: RATP app for transit_

### Accommodation Areas
- **Le Marais**: Central & historic
- **Latin Quarter**: Academic vibe
- **Canal Saint-Martin**: Hip area
- _Book ahead: Peak seasons fill quickly_

### Budget Tips
- Museums: Free first Sunday
- Lunch menus: Better value
